01.2 / ACCURACY BY TIER

The mid-round dead zone

Group players by what ESPN projected them for, and a pattern shows up: the 9–12 PPG band — classic rounds 4 through 7 draft territory — is where projections go to die.

By projected PPG tier

Beat rate per tier

Beat rate = the share of players in a tier who scored more per game than ESPN projected. 50% is a coin flip; below that, the tier underdelivered more often than not.

The takeaway Only 24% of players ESPN projected for 9–12 PPG beat their number — versus 53% of cheap fliers in the 3–6 band. ESPN bakes optimism into the mid-rounds and caution into the bargain bin.

01.4 / DO WEEKLY PROJECTIONS SHARPEN?

The miss you can't shake

A reasonable hunch: as the season builds a track record, ESPN's weekly projections should tighten up. They don't. Each box is one week's spread of absolute error — and there's a floor of miss that more data simply can't drill through. A projection is a likely outcome, not a crystal ball; any single game is one noisy draw from a range.

Absolute error per week

Weekly miss distribution · all players

Absolute error = how many points the weekly projection missed by, up or down. The line in each box is the median miss; the box holds the middle 50% of players; whiskers reach the rest. The amber line tracks each week's average miss.

The hypothesis didn't hold The boxes don't shrink — they hold flat all season. The typical weekly miss stays near 3.5–4 points from Week 1 to Week 18, and the average miss actually drifts slightly upward (4.6 early vs. 5.0 late). More games on the books didn't sharpen the calls; it just confirmed there's an irreducible floor of miss in every weekly projection.
02.1 / GROUND RULE

Injuries break the math first

Before crowning any bust, separate "played badly" from "didn't play." A preseason projection assumes a full season; miss a third of it and the season total collapses no matter how good the player is. These are the audited players who missed four or more games — the misses ESPN had no way to price in.

Read PPG carefully Every per-game number on this dashboard is points ÷ games actually played, not spread across 17 weeks. So a player can post a strong PPG and still wreck your season at a roster spot — the rate held up, the availability didn't. Watch the per-game column below: some of these names were still excellent when on the field.

04 / DATA ROOM

The whole board

Every audited player, projected vs. actual, with consistency built in. Sort any column. The point of the consistency columns: two players can land at the same average for very different reasons — a steady grinder and a boom-or-bust gamble look identical until you see the spread.

How to read consistency CV (coefficient of variation) is week-to-week volatility scaled to a player's own average, so positions compare fairly — lower is steadier. Boom/Bust count games far above or below their typical output. A low CV with a fat actual total is the gold standard: reliable points every week.
⌕

Consistency tier is shaded from the column itself: steady average volatile, split at the league's CV quartiles. Boom = a game ≥ 1.5× the player's average; bust = a game ≤ 0.5×.

RULE 01

RULE 03

Two of every three receivers fell short of their number. Receiver projections carried the most systematic optimism in 2025.

Players projected for 3–6 PPG beat their numbers more than half the time. Late-round fliers were the market's best value.
